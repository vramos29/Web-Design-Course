Supporting Law Enforcement

This page is dedicated to reasons why law enforcement should be supported, locally and federally.

Law Enforcement

Since the formation of modern civilization, different forms of law enforcement have existed and adapted over time to suit the evolved changes of society. Think back to some of the earliest forms of this system that you can think of: Roman centurions, royal guards, sheriffs and marshals, and, of course, modern day police and federal officers. These brave people are not meant to inflict harm onto civilians, but rather protect them from danger and those deemed to pose threats. This task, which is not an easy burden to carry, is done so by those with the strength to face death every single day they enter the field. This article will discuss why law enforcement should be supported, not defunded or abolished; because, in the end, without law enforcement, there would be no one to protect us from the same dangers they face every single day.

Reasons Why Law Enforcement Is Necessary

  1. Safety: Without Law Enforcement, communities would be left without protection
  2. Justice: Law Enforcement help ensure our nation's Constitution is upheld
  3. Sacrifice: Law Enforcement is responsible for ensuring civilian safety above their own

Imagine there was a situation where a family was home and someone broke into their house. They would not call the fire department or the ambulence. They would call the police to come and safe them. More times than not law enforcement officers are looked down upon for their line of work because people do not recgonize the sacrifices they make every single day to ensure communal safety.
